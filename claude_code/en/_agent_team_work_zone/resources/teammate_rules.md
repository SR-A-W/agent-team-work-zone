## Teammate Rules (essentials)

1. **Only touch your own workstation** — your workstation is `<team>/teammates/<your-name>/`; only you maintain its 5 files. **Do not** modify another teammate's, the lead's, or anyone else's workstation files; to get someone else to do something, send them a SendMessage. Peer-to-peer communication and collaboration (asking questions, sharing, challenging, helping each other) is encouraged and is a core value of the team; but formal task assignment and prioritization is the lead's coordination responsibility — don't hand tasks to peers as if they were orders.
2. **Cross-agent communication follows Claude Code's official agent-team mechanism (mailbox / SendMessage)** — per the official mechanism, agents communicate only via mailbox delivery (the SendMessage tool); your plain output **does not cross the agent boundary** to reach the lead — it exists only in your own session, visible only if a human user looks at your pane/transcript. Reporting progress, asking questions, delivering work **must** go through SendMessage, or it didn't happen. (When you go idle, the system notifies the lead automatically, but that's a content-free heartbeat — it **cannot substitute** for your report.)
3. **Checkpointing is a standing obligation** — after task completion / before going idle / when prompted → call `/checkpoint` to update `working-context.md`. It's your handoff note to your next-spawned self; Claude Code does **not** preserve teammate sessions across restarts, so a poorly written one means the next you can't recover. `commitments.md` holds your promises to others — the next you must pick those up. Don't rely solely on the 15-minute automatic reminder as a backstop.
4. **After compression, recover from your workstation files, not memory** — after context compression, read your own workstation to recover state: `README.md` (role identity), `working-context.md` (work state), `commitments.md` (outstanding promises), `TODO.md` (pending items). Don't guess from residual memory.
5. **Task tracking lives on disk in your own workstation** — `TODO.md` / `ACTIVE_JOBS.md` / `COMPLETED_JOBS.md` belong in your workstation directory; **do not** use `~/.claude/tasks/` (session-scoped — gone the moment the conversation ends).
6. **When in doubt, ask the lead — not the user directly** — if requirements, purpose, or direction are unclear, use SendMessage to ask the team lead; the lead relays genuinely major questions to the user. **A wrong assumption costs far more than one extra question**; **never pretend you already have agreement**. Also **don't stop and wait idle for the user to reply** — the user usually only watches the lead's session (or is in remote-control/non-tmux mode) and can't see your question at all; you waiting on the user while the lead waits on your deliverable is a whole-team deadlock.
7. **What goes into roundtable** — reports must be self-contained; filename format `<your-name>_<type>_<YYYYMMDD>_<HHMM>_<description>.md`; **archiving rights belong solely to the issuer** — if you didn't post it, don't archive it; if you did post it and every recipient has marked it RESOLVED, you archive it.
